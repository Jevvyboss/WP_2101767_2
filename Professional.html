Job and Requirements

Cyber Security Analyst

 A cyber security analyst is primarily responsible for protecting a company’s network and systems from cyber attacks. This involves researching upcoming IT trends, creating contingency plans, reviewing suspicious activities, reporting security breaches, and educating the rest of the company on security measures.

  1. Education and Certifications
    • Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Computer Science, Information Technology, Cybersecurity, or a related field.
    • Relevant certifications such as CompTIA Security+, Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH), etc.
  2. Knowledge and Skills
    • Proficiency in network protocols, security protocols, and various operating systems.
    • Understanding of security frameworks and best practices (e.g., NIST, ISO 27001, CIS Controls).
    • Knowledge of encryption technologies, firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and other security tools.
    • Strong understanding of threat intelligence, vulnerability management, and incident response.
    • Programming and scripting skills in languages like Python, PowerShell, or Ruby.
  3. Experience
    • 1-3 years of experience in cybersecurity roles (e.g., security analyst, system administrator, network engineer) depending on the specific position.
    • Experience in security monitoring, incident analysis, and security operations.
  4. Analytical and Problem-Solving Skills
    • Ability to analyze complex data and trends to identify potential security threats and vulnerabilities.
    • Aptitude for critical thinking and problem-solving in a dynamic and fast-paced environment.
  5. Communication and Collaboration
    • Excellent written and verbal communication skills to effectively interact with team members, clients, and stakeholders.
    • Collaboration skills to work effectively in multidisciplinary teams and articulate security risks and recommendations to non-technical stakeholders.
