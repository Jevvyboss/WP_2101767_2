My Degree

 On my academic journey, I am diligently working towards a degree in Computer Networking and Cybersecurity, a field that intrigues me profoundly.

Skills I have learnt:

  • Advanced Network Configuration and Management:

    •  In my third year, I have gained expertise in configuring and managing complex computer networks. This includes setting up routers, switches, firewalls, and other network devices, as well as optimizing network performance and ensuring security measures are appropriately implemented.

  • Cybersecurity Analysis and Incident Response:

    •  I have developed the ability to analyze potential cybersecurity threats and vulnerabilities. This includes identifying security breaches, conducting risk assessments, and devising incident response plans to mitigate and recover from cyber-attacks effectively. Understanding various attack vectors and defense mechanisms is a crucial aspect of this skill.

  • Ethical Hacking and Penetration Testing:

    •  As a third-year student, I have been exposed to ethical hacking techniques and penetration testing methodologies. This skill involves identifying system weaknesses and vulnerabilities through authorized simulated cyber-attacks. By understanding how attackers operate, Jevon can better design and implement robust security measures to protect systems and networks.

12 Pillar

 After diving into the chapters discussing goals, time management, and the importance of continuous learning, I am eager to summarize and reflect on these valuable insights and envision how they can shape my life. Firstly, the idea that formal education is merely a stepping stone resonates deeply with me. Education is a lifelong journey, extending beyond the classroom. As I start my college adventure, I see the importance of continuously educating myself, not only academically but in all aspects of life. This perspective motivates me to actively seek knowledge beyond my prescribed syllabus, explore various subjects, and cultivate a habit of reading and learning outside of the academic realm.
 Secondly, the significance of effective goal setting is a principle I plan to apply diligently. College life can be overwhelming, and having clear short-term, mid-term, and long-term goals will provide me with direction and purpose. I intend to define specific academic, personal, and career-related objectives. Breaking them down into achievable milestones will help me track my progress and stay motivated throughout this transformative journey.
 Additionally, mastering time management is crucial in the hectic life of a college student. Balancing classes, assignments, extracurricular activities, and personal time demands effective scheduling and prioritization. I aspire to prioritize tasks based on their importance and align them with my goals. This way, I can make the most of my time and maintain a healthy balance between academics, personal growth, and relaxation.
 Furthermore, building a network of inspiring individuals and seeking mentorship is something I recognize as pivotal. College is a hub of opportunities to connect with diverse and motivated individuals. Engaging with them, attending workshops, and seeking guidance from mentors can broaden my perspectives and nurture a supportive community, which is fundamental for personal and professional growth.
 Lastly, the idea of regular self-reflection and progress evaluation is something I aim to incorporate into my routine. College is a time of immense growth and self-discovery. By periodically reflecting on my experiences, learning from both successes and failures, and adjusting my strategies accordingly, I can continuously improve and stay on track towards achieving my aspirations.
 In summary, these insights have given me a roadmap to navigate college and beyond. They remind me that education doesn't end with a degree but is a lifelong pursuit. Effective goal setting, time management, meaningful connections, and self-reflection are the building blocks for a successful and fulfilling journey through college and into the future.
